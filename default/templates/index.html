About

Who am I?

My name is Andy Keller and I was born in Regensburg, Germany to a German father and a Vietnamese mother. A couple months after I turned three, we moved to Richmond, Virginia and I have lived there ever since. As I grew up, my parents patiently taught me both of their mother tongues and I even picked up a bit of French in high school.

Academic Career

After highschool I entered the University of Virginia and majored in systems engineering at the School of Engineering and Applied Science (SEAS). During my second semester, the Rodman Scholars Program, which consists of the top 5% of students enrolled in SEAS, inducted me into their prestigious organization. Then, in my third semester, I went on to found the Math Modeling Group at UVA, which promotes the practical application of course work on real world problems.

Professional Career

Last summer, I participated in an absolutely amazing internship program with Epic Systems Corporation. Epic is a privately held health care software company whose systems are installed in major hospitals and hold the medical records of almost half the patients in the United States. I recently accepted Epic's fulltime offer and will be starting as a software developer in their analytics devision at the end of next summer.

Projects

Capstone: Google Glass in Surgery

I am working with Professor Stephanie Guerlain and Doctor James Forrest Calland on developing applications for Google Glass in surgery. Our primary goal is to discover use cases for Glass that can be utilized in the operating room in order to assist medical students in actively learning how to perform a surgery. We will also place emphasis on developing applications that assist in the standardization of surgical procedures in order to improve safety in the operating room.

Thesis: What is Google up to?

I am researching Google's business model and attempting to determine what Google's plans are for the forseeable future. In a recent article, the Wall Street Journal implies that Google is in direct competition with Apple with its new Nexus 6 smartphone. I, on the otherhand, do not believe that Google is competing directly with Apple at all. Furthmore, I claim that Google identifies itself purely as a software company that sells advertisements. Any foray into hardware, operating systems, or another industry is merely an attempt to increase advertisement viewership.

Predictive Technology Laboratory

I assisted Dr. Matthew Gerber of the Predictive Technology Laboratory (PTL) in developing two open source software projects: an open research platform and a crime prediction platform. The open research platform allows researchers and data scientists to access PTL datasets and compete with each other to develop the best forecaster. The crime prediction platform (Asymmmetric Threat Tracker) will allow municipalities to input marginal data in order to predict where crimes are most likely to occur.
